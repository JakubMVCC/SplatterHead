How to Make Your Own Armature Guide

So, what exactly is an armature? Think of it as the skeleton inside your claymation character. Usually made from aluminium wire, it provides strong support and flexibility, allowing your figure to hold poses, move smoothly, and stand up without collapsing or sagging under the weight of the clay. This is important especially during the long, frame by frame animation process. Without this internal structure, your clay creation might crack, deform, or simply fall apart, making animation incredibly difficult. Basically, the armature acts as the bones, while the clay is the skin, holding the character's shape consistently as you make those tiny adjustments for each shot.
